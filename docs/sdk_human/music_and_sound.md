# Music & Sound in Aurex-X (Human Guide)

Aurex-X makes music and voices from math, not samples.

## What you can make

- Beats and basslines
- Leads and pads
- Robotic/alien vowel vocals
- Audio-reactive visuals (lights, fog, motion)

## Building blocks

- **Synth Graph**: virtual modules like oscillator, filter, delay, distortion.
- **Sequencer**: notes in patterns at a BPM.
- **Voice Synth**: phoneme-based vocal tones (`AH`, `EH`, `OH`, etc.).
- **Audio Analysis**: kick/bass/mid/high energies used by visuals.

## Creative workflow

1. Pick BPM and one track.
2. Add a simple synth graph.
3. Add a short phoneme phrase.
4. Route audio energy into fields/lights.
5. Add timeline choreography.

## Example ideas

- **Prime Pulse Anthem**: tunnel + kick pulses + robot voice.
- **Psytrance Dimension**: flow fields + bass-reactive fog.
- **Circuit Rhythm World**: component glow synced to kick/snare.

## Why this matters

You can generate complete audiovisual demos from JSON only.
No external audio files required.

## Current limitations (so expectations are clear)

- The note sequencer mostly drives **energy and groove** right now, not fully realistic instrument playback.
- Effects like filter/reverb/delay are intentionally simplified and deterministic.
- Voice is intentionally synthetic (vowel-like tones), not natural text-to-speech.

## Practical tips

- Use the synth graph for the actual tone/character.
- Use tracks to create beat energy that pushes lights, fog, and motion.
- Use voice presets for texture and identity, not intelligible lyrics.
