# Cinematic World Presentation

Aurex-X now supports camera choreography so scenes feel directed rather than static.

## What changed for creators
- Orbit and flythrough camera styles for world reveal.
- Beat- and phrase-driven movement accents.
- Automatic shot sequencing for complex procedural scenes.
- Film-like finishing (tone mapping, vignette, bloom behavior, grain).
- Stronger light shafts in fog-heavy worlds.

## Why it matters
Procedural scenes can be visually dense. Camera direction helps the viewer understand scale, rhythm, and detail.

## Authoring mindset
- Use flythroughs for tunnels and forward momentum.
- Use orbits for architecture/fractal forms.
- Use slower movement for high pattern density.
- Keep volumetric effects subtle enough to preserve emissive highlights.
